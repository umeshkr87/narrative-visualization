One of the major deciding factors of how much meat people eat is how rich they are. Scatter plot shows meat consumption per person per year (y-axis) and average GDP per capita (x-axis). We see a strong relationship: richer a country, more meat the average person eats. Higher GDP Per Capita countries such as Hong Kong, United Stated have higher meat consumption than the countries with lower GDP as clearly shown from the plot.
